# Deep Research: DF and DD willingness to tolerate a blue government

Date: 31 March 2026

## Context

Our coalition formation model currently gives blue-bloc coalitions ~35%
combined probability. The main blue pathways are V+KF+LA+M (~10%),
V+KF+M (~11%), and V+LA+M (~7%). None of these include DF or DD as
government parties — they all depend on DF and/or DD either **actively
tolerating** or **passively abstaining** on confidence votes.

The model currently sets these bilateral values:

- DF→V tolerateInGov: 0.90
- DF→KF tolerateInGov: 0.80
- DF→LA tolerateInGov: 0.75
- DF→M tolerateInGov: 0.20
- DD→V tolerateInGov: 0.85
- DD→KF tolerateInGov: 0.78
- DD→LA tolerateInGov: 0.75
- DD→M tolerateInGov: 0.30

The high DF/DD tolerance toward V, KF, and LA is what keeps blue
coalitions alive. But commentators increasingly describe blue chances
as near-zero, and Moderaterne's Mohammad Rona declared on 30 March that
the blue bloc is "helt finito." If commentators are right and we're
wrong, the disconnect is most likely in these tolerance values.

## Research question

**How realistic is it that DF and DD would let a V-led government pass
that includes Moderaterne, without DF/DD being in government themselves?**

## What to look for

Search DR, TV2, Altinget, Information, Berlingske, Politiken, Jyllands-
Posten, and party social media for evidence on each of the following.
For each item, report what you found or explicitly state "no evidence
found."

### 1. DF's post-election posture on blue government tolerance

- Has Morten Messerschmidt said anything **after 24 March** about
  whether DF would tolerate a blue government it's not part of?
- Has DF set conditions or demands for tolerance/abstention?
- Has DF indicated it would **block** a blue government to force new
  elections or a different formation?
- Any statements about DF's willingness to let Løkke participate in a
  blue government? (DF→M tolerance is the critical bottleneck.)

### 2. DD's post-election posture on blue government tolerance

- Has Inger Støjberg said anything **after 24 March** about whether DD
  would tolerate a blue government it's not part of?
- Has DD set conditions for tolerance? Policy demands?
- Any signals about DD's view of M's role in a blue coalition?

### 3. DF-M and DD-M relationship dynamics

- Any evidence of personal or political hostility between
  Messerschmidt/Støjberg and Løkke that would make tolerance harder?
- Historical precedent: in 2022-2025, did DF/DD indicate willingness
  to support M-inclusive governments?
- Any commentary on whether DF/DD see strategic value in blocking blue
  to get a better deal (new elections, pressure on S, etc.)?

### 4. The "free tolerance" question

- Is there any evidence that DF or DD would hand V a government **for
  free** — i.e., tolerate without extracting policy concessions?
- Or is the consensus that tolerance would require substantial
  concessions (immigration, EU, etc.) that might be incompatible with
  M's participation?
- Commentator analysis of whether the DF/DD tolerance assumption is
  realistic or whether it's treated as a fantasy scenario.

### 5. Structural barriers to blue tolerance

- Would DF and DD voters accept their parties enabling a government
  they're excluded from?
- Is there a competitive dynamic where DF and DD would rather keep
  each other out of influence than cooperate on tolerance?
- Does the BP fallout affect DF/DD calculus? (Both are right-wing
  parties that might be tainted by association.)

## What NOT to do

- Do NOT speculate. If there's no evidence, say so.
- Do NOT propose parameter values — that's the analyst's job.
- Focus on direct quotes, attributed statements, and sourced analysis.
- Cover the full post-election period (24 March onward), not just the
  last 24 hours.
