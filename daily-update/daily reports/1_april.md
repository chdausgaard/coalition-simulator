# Research Brief: 1 April 2026

## Summary
Fresh coalition-relevant reporting in the valid window pointed mainly to movement on Mette Frederiksen’s support side rather than to a public breakthrough among the core Danish government parties. At Marienborg on 31 March, Greenland’s Naaja Nathanielsen said IA “obviously” leans red and that she had difficulty seeing a viable blue constellation, while also attaching Greenlandic foreign-policy demands to the talks; Ritzau also reported that Sjúrður Skaale arrived pointing to Frederiksen. Separately, Enhedslisten’s Pelle Dragsted said on the way into negotiations that EL would press its demands without issuing ultimatums and accepted that all sides would need to bend. Same-window reporting also shows the process is now in continuing negotiations at Marienborg, not just initial set-up meetings.

## Developments

### 1. North Atlantic signals at Marienborg favored Frederiksen over a blue route
**What:** On arrival for talks with Mette Frederiksen at Marienborg on 31 March, IA’s Naaja Nathanielsen publicly leaned toward a red outcome and said she struggled to see how a blue government would come together. In the same reporting cluster, she also tied the meeting to a demand for more Greenlandic room in foreign policy.

**Quote:** “Vi hælder selvfølgelig rødt.” / “Lige nu har jeg også svært ved at se, hvordan konstellationen skal være, hvis det skulle være en blå regering.” — Herald/Ritzau, 31 March 2026.

**Responses:** The same same-window Ritzau reporting added a second North Atlantic signal: Sjúrður Skaale was reported, on arrival at Marienborg, to be pointing to Mette Frederiksen. Nathanielsen’s own line was paired with a substantive Greenlandic ask on foreign policy rather than a simple endorsement.

**Coalition impact:** This makes a blue route look harder to assemble politically on the North Atlantic flank and improves Frederiksen’s room to negotiate an S-led outcome. But it also suggests that any North Atlantic help is likely to be conditional: Nathanielsen explicitly linked her participation to more Greenlandic authority and voice in foreign affairs, especially on Arctic matters.

### 2. Enhedslisten entered talks signaling flexibility on tactics, not on policy preferences
**What:** In reporting published on the morning of 1 April about Tuesday’s talks, Pelle Dragsted said Enhedslisten still wants a wealth tax, but he emphasized that the party is not entering the negotiations with ultimatums and accepts that all sides will need to bend.

**Quote:** “We’re going into these negotiations with our own demands. But we understand that everyone is going to need to bend towards each other. And we’re prepared to do that,” while not “setting any ultimatums on anything.” — The Local/Ritzau, 1 April 2026.

**Responses:** No same-window party response was quoted in the item. The report presented Dragsted’s remarks as his line on the way into the negotiations themselves.

**Coalition impact:** This is a clear softening in bargaining style, not a retreat on substance. EL kept the wealth-tax demand alive, but Dragsted signaled lower tactical harshness and a greater willingness to avoid becoming the immediate blocker. That marginally eases the path for S-led formats that would need EL as a support or tolerance actor.

## Formation stage
Current stage: forhandlinger. Same-window reporting states that “Regeringsforhandlinger fortsætter tirsdag og onsdag på Marienborg” after Friday’s initial sættemøder; Tuesday’s schedule included bilateral meetings with the North Atlantic MPs and a separate Enhedslisten meeting at 14:00.

## Key signals to watch
The next key signals are whether Nathanielsen’s red-leaning line hardens into an explicit support commitment; whether Greenland’s demand for more foreign-policy voice becomes an overt condition in the Danish talks; whether other North Atlantic MPs echo Skaale’s pro-Frederiksen position or dilute it; and whether EL’s no-ultimatum posture survives concrete bargaining on tax and housing. The highest-value missing public signal remains any fresh same-window quote from SF or Moderaterne on whether they can actually coexist in government, because today’s verified movement came from the North Atlantic flank and EL instead.
