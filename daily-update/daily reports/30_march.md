# Research Brief: 31 March 2026

## Scope note
This version expands the earlier brief into full markdown using only the material already gathered. It does **not** add new reporting beyond what was already in hand, and it remains restricted to developments reported during **30 March 2026** or the **morning of 31 March 2026**.

## Summary
During 30 March and the morning of 31 March, the clearest new movement was negative for any blue-led route that would need Borgernes Parti or its orbit as a usable parliamentary base. Lars Boje Mathiesen refused to distance himself from Nadja Natalie Isaksen's line on Samira Nawa, and later the same day he was still firefighting the Jacob Harris affair while admitting more damaging information could still emerge. At the same time, Greenland's two new MPs arrived in Copenhagen signaling a coordinated posture ahead of Tuesday's Marienborg meeting. Net effect: blue arithmetic looked harder to operationalize, while the leverage of actors outside the normal red/blue bloc logic increased.

## Developments

### 1. Borgernes Parti controversy widened around Samira Nawa

**What happened**

On 30 March, Lars Boje Mathiesen was asked to respond after Nadja Natalie Isaksen had questioned whether Samira Nawa was Danish. Boje declined to engage the substance of the issue and said he would not address it that day. The exchange mattered because it pushed BP's parliamentary usability back into the news during a live coalition-formation phase.

**Direct quotes**

> “Det kommer jeg ikke til at forholde mig til i dag. Det er noget politisk. Jeg har af givne årsager ikke haft tid til at kigge på alt det politiske lige nu.”
>
> — Lars Boje Mathiesen, quoted by Ritzau via Avisen.dk, 30 March 2026

> “Det er et synspunkt, som alene hører til i historiebøgerne, og jeg synes, Isaksen bør skrue gevaldigt ned for charmen.”
>
> — Rasmus Stoklund, quoted by Ritzau via Avisen.dk, 30 March 2026

**How parties responded**

- **Borgernes Parti / Lars Boje Mathiesen:** Boje did not disavow the underlying statement. His response was procedural and evasive rather than corrective.
- **Socialdemokratiet / Rasmus Stoklund:** Stoklund responded sharply and framed Isaksen's position as beyond the pale.
- **Other parties:** No additional same-window party reaction was included in the already gathered material.

**Coalition impact**

This development did not change formal seat counts, but it raised the reputational and transactional cost of relying on BP-linked mandates in a governing arrangement. That matters most for any blue-led project requiring outside tolerance or a broad parliamentary basis. It also creates friction for Moderaterne, since a blue path becomes harder to sell if it appears indirectly dependent on BP's orbit. The effect is therefore negative for blue coalition pathways that already depended on tolerance from unstable or controversial actors.

**Source**
- Ritzau via Avisen.dk, 30 March 2026

### 2. Jacob Harris fallout deepened BP's instability

**What happened**

Later on 30 March, Ritzau reported that Borgernes Parti had been warned months earlier about the now-expelled Jacob Harris. In response, Lars Boje Mathiesen said more damaging information might still emerge, while also trying to close the matter and move on.

**Direct quote**

> “Og hvem ved, om der kommer endnu mere. Så for os i Borgernes Parti er det sådan set dejligt, at vi kan lukke bogen og så kigge frem på det arbejde, vi skal lave.”
>
> — Lars Boje Mathiesen, quoted by Ritzau via Avisen.dk, 30 March 2026

**How parties responded**

- **Borgernes Parti / Lars Boje Mathiesen:** Boje acknowledged uncertainty about whether more revelations could appear, while simultaneously trying to signal closure.
- **Other parties:** No further same-window cross-party reaction was included in the material already gathered.

**Coalition impact**

This further weakened BP's reliability as a parliamentary actor. For coalition formation, the issue is not only ideological compatibility but operational dependability. Any blue coalition route that needs BP-adjacent support now looks riskier, because the parliamentary base appears both politically toxic and organizationally unstable. Relative to that, non-BP-dependent arrangements become easier to defend. The effect is therefore negative for blue scenarios resting on a fragmented right flank.

**Source**
- Ritzau via Avisen.dk, 30 March 2026

### 3. Greenland's two new MPs arrived with a coordinated line before Marienborg

**What happened**

On the morning of 31 March, Sermitsiaq reported that Naaja H. Nathanielsen and Qarsoq Høegh-Dam had arrived in Copenhagen on Monday, gone directly to Christiansborg, and were preparing for Tuesday morning's meeting at Marienborg. Both indicated that Greenland would approach the talks in a coordinated way.

**Direct quotes**

> “Det lyder til, at Grønland står mere samlet i forhold til forhandlingerne end Danmark gør.”
>
> — Qarsoq Høegh-Dam, Sermitsiaq liveblog entry, morning of 31 March 2026

> “Vi har jo drøftet nogle overordnede linjer og er enige om, hvad vi skal have på bordet fra grønlandsk side.”
>
> — Naaja H. Nathanielsen, Sermitsiaq liveblog entry, morning of 31 March 2026

**How parties responded**

- **Naaja H. Nathanielsen and Qarsoq Høegh-Dam:** Both signaled coordination and a shared Greenlandic negotiating position.
- **Danish party leaders:** No same-window direct response from Danish party leaders was included in the already gathered material.

**Coalition impact**

This did not amount to a public endorsement of either bloc, but it increased the leverage of the North Atlantic seats by showing coordination instead of divergence. That matters because close parliamentary arithmetic makes small actors more pivotal, especially when they act collectively. Any narrow coalition path now has to take Greenlandic priorities more seriously and earlier. This tends to favor formations that can manage high-trust bargaining and realm politics credibly, while making already fragile low-margin pathways harder to execute.

**Source**
- Sermitsiaq liveblog, morning of 31 March 2026

## Cross-development assessment

Across the material already gathered, the pattern runs in one direction:

1. **Blue-bloc execution costs rose.** BP became less usable as either a direct support party or an indirect part of a respectable parliamentary basis.
2. **Moderaterne's blue option looked less clean.** Even without a direct new Moderaterne quote in the gathered material, the surrounding parliamentary environment became less attractive for a blue-oriented strategy.
3. **Peripheral actors gained leverage.** Greenland's coordinated posture mattered because close arithmetic makes small, organized actors more consequential.

## Formation stage
Current stage: **forhandlinger**.

More specifically, the process appears to be in bilateral and exploratory bargaining, with Tuesday morning talks at Marienborg involving the Greenlandic MPs.

## Key signals to watch

- Whether **Moderaterne** comment on the BP/Harris/Isaksen fallout in explicitly coalition-related terms.
- Whether the Greenlandic MPs continue acting as a **joint negotiating package** after the Marienborg meeting.
- Whether any party begins distinguishing between **formal coalition participation** and **parliamentary tolerance**, which would reveal adaptation to the tougher blue arithmetic.
