# Research Brief: 9–13 April 2026

## Summary

By late afternoon on 9 April, the active process still ran on the existing center-left-plus-Moderaterne track: Socialdemokratiet said Friday's talks would continue with SF, Radikale, Enhedslisten, Alternativet, and Moderaterne. The real break came on 10 April, when Troels Lund Poulsen publicly nudged Lars Løkke Rasmussen toward a blue-leaning economic track and Løkke answered with an ultimatum: no more Moderaterne participation unless Venstre and Konservative were brought into the real negotiations. That demand worked procedurally. Venstre accepted a Monday meeting while stressing that an S-led government still did not look "oplagt," De Konservative were then scheduled for Tuesday, and on 12 April Mette Frederiksen publicly opened a broad government "hen over midten" while explicitly keeping the center-left track alive. By the morning of 13 April, however, the core compatibility problem remained unresolved: Venstre still said an S-led government was not an obvious fit, while SF publicly ruled out governing with Venstre or Konservative, and Radikale stayed open both ways. ([Ni.dk][1])

## Developments

### 10 April

#### 1. Troels Lund's outreach triggered Løkke's ultimatum and forced the table open to Venstre and Konservative

**What:** Early on 10 April, Troels Lund Poulsen used social media to urge Lars Løkke Rasmussen to prioritize policy over personalities and avoid a government dependent on Enhedslisten. A few hours later, Løkke turned that into a formal bargaining move: Moderaterne would stop attending, beyond the planned Friday meeting, unless Mette Frederiksen expanded the talks to include Venstre and De Konservative. By Friday evening, Løkke said he was "bekræftet i" that Frederiksen would now reach out; by late Friday and then Saturday, Venstre's Monday meeting and Konservative's Tuesday meeting were confirmed. ([Ni.dk][2])
**Quote:** "Vi kommer ikke til at deltage i flere møder med hende som kongelig undersøger, ud over det i eftermiddag, medmindre hun tager den opgave på sig." — Lars Løkke Rasmussen, Ritzau, 10 April 2026, 13:31. ([Ni.dk][3])
**Responses:** Troels Lund had written that Løkke should let it handle "noget" rather than "nogen," and warned against being part of "en regering, der er afhængig af Enhedslistens hovedbestyrelse." Later the same day, Løkke said Frederiksen was reaching out to V and K; that night Stephanie Lose confirmed Venstre would attend on Monday, and on 11 April Ritzau reported that De Konservative had accepted Tuesday's invitation. ([Ni.dk][2])
**Coalition impact:** This was the window's clearest process shock. It made the existing center-left-plus-M path harder to continue as the only active track, and it made S-led cross-bloc formats involving Venstre, and potentially Konservative, procedurally live rather than merely speculative. It also showed that Løkke was using Moderaterne's participation itself as leverage over the negotiation format. ([Ni.dk][3])

#### 2. Venstre agreed to enter talks, but only on explicitly hard economic terms

**What:** After the Løkke ultimatum, Venstre did not refuse the invitation. Instead, Stephanie Lose said the party would attend Monday's meeting, while making clear that attendance did not equal political convergence. Her post framed economic responsibility and reform policy as Venstre's entry condition. ([LinkedIn][4])
**Quote:** "For nuværende virker det fortsat ikke oplagt, at Socialdemokratiet kan og vil være en del af en regering, der leverer det, som er en afgørende forudsætning for Venstre." — Stephanie Lose, LinkedIn / quoted by Ritzau, 10 April 2026. ([LinkedIn][4])
**Responses:** In the same post, Lose said Venstre had urged Løkke that morning to seek the role of kongelig undersøger for Venstre, "med Troels for bordenden," and Troels Lund's own earlier message to Løkke had likewise pressed for "en økonomisk ansvarlig kurs." ([LinkedIn][4])
**Coalition impact:** Venstre's move widened the negotiating space but did not signal acceptance of an S-led government. The broad-middle route became more real procedurally, yet Venstre simultaneously raised the price of entry by defining the dispute as one about the government's economic foundation. That made any S+M+V-type arrangement more feasible institutionally, but not clearly easier politically. ([LinkedIn][4])

### 12 April

#### 3. Frederiksen publicly opened the broad-middle track while refusing to abandon center-left

**What:** On Sunday, Mette Frederiksen said publicly that she would test whether "en bred regering hen over midten" was possible, even as she insisted that a center-left government still had potential. This was the first clear same-window public signal from Frederiksen herself that the broadened track forced open on Friday was now an official line, not just a response to pressure. ([Ni.dk][5])
**Quote:** "Samtidig intensiverer vi drøftelserne også med Venstre og Konservative. Særligt om økonomi. Begge partier har klart sagt, at de helst vil en ren borgerlig politik. Uden at definere, hvad det egentlig betyder politisk. Men jeg tror, danskerne forventer en vis pragmatisme fra os alle sammen." — Mette Frederiksen, Facebook / Ritzau, 12 April 2026, 15:25. ([Ni.dk][5])
**Responses:** Frederiksen's post came after Friday's Moderaterne ultimatum had already produced Monday and Tuesday invitations for Venstre and Konservative. In the same post, she also looked back positively on SVM — "jeg var glad for den" — and wrote that the question was whether something like that could be done "igen og med endnu flere partier." ([Ni.dk][5])
**Coalition impact:** This kept two routes alive at once. It improved the standing of S-led broad-middle formulas by making them an explicit Frederiksen objective, not just a Løkke demand; but because she simultaneously said the center-left route still had "potentiale," it did not close the S+SF+RV+M direction. The result was greater formal flexibility from S, not a definitive choice. ([Ni.dk][5])

### 13 April (morning)

#### 4. Venstre went into Monday's talks still saying an S-led government was not "oplagt"

**What:** On the morning the Monday talks resumed, Troels Lund Poulsen reiterated that Venstre still did not see an obvious shared economic foundation with Socialdemokratiet. He also argued that the new Folketing is "mere blåt" than the last one and that the next government should reflect that. ([Ni.dk][6])
**Quote:** "Derfor har jeg også sagt, at det ikke er oplagt, at vi kan lægge et fælles fundament for en økonomisk politik. Det mener jeg fortsat." — Troels Lund Poulsen, Ritzau quoting social media, 13 April 2026, 09:05. ([Ni.dk][6])
**Responses:** The same Ritzau report noted that Troels Lund's earlier outreach had been read by Løkke as an opening for real talks under Socialdemokratiet-led negotiations, but Lund's new morning message narrowed that opening again by emphasizing both economic distance and a more blue parliament. SF responded later that morning by arguing that Lund wants "en endnu mere blå regering" than SVM. ([Ni.dk][6])
**Coalition impact:** This matters because it distinguishes attendance from softness. Venstre was willing to negotiate, but not yet willing to normalize a new S-led arrangement. That keeps S+M+V and broader S-led cross-bloc formats alive procedurally, while still leaving their substantive basis unresolved and costly. ([Ni.dk][6])

#### 5. SF drew a firm public veto against governing with Venstre or Konservative, while Radikale stayed open both ways

**What:** As talks resumed Monday, Pia Olsen Dyhr said SF would not enter government with Venstre or De Konservative, even though those parties had now been pulled into the expanded talks at Moderaterne's insistence. In the same same-window reporting, Martin Lidegaard said Radikale remained open to either a red government or a middle government, both under Socialdemokratiet. ([Ni.dk][7])
**Quote:** "SF kommer ikke til at gå i regering med Venstre eller De Konservative." — Pia Olsen Dyhr, Ritzau, 13 April 2026, 11:57. ([Ni.dk][7])
**Responses:** Pia Olsen Dyhr also said there is "alt for langt fra Venstre til SF," and warned that SF could bend in talks but would not "flække sig selv på tværs." Martin Lidegaard, by contrast, said: "Vi skal have afklaring … vi er åbne for at forhandle begge veje," and added that he wanted clarity "inden for dage og ikke uger." ([Ni.dk][7])
**Coalition impact:** This was the strongest same-window anti-coalition signal from the red side. It sharply reduced the plausibility of any S-led government that tries to combine SF with Venstre or Konservative in cabinet. It kept an S-led center-left route alive only if Moderaterne can be integrated without forcing SF to swallow V/K as government partners. At the same time, RV's two-track openness preserved some maneuvering room for Frederiksen if she continues to hedge between center-left and broad-middle options. ([Ni.dk][7])

## Formation stage

Current stage: forhandlinger. The process is past the initial sættemøder and is now an expanded round of active government talks in Statsministeriet, with Venstre brought in on Monday and De Konservative scheduled for Tuesday. ([Ni.dk][8])

## Key signals to watch

The next things to monitor are whether Venstre's participation produces any softening beyond the current economic-precondition language; whether Moderaterne treat the widened table as enough or push harder for a genuine broad-middle government; whether Frederiksen eventually closes either the center-left or broad-middle track instead of keeping both alive; whether SF's veto on V/K in government remains absolute once bargaining intensifies; and whether RV's "begge veje" posture becomes pivotal if S tries to build a government without resolving the SF–M compatibility problem first.
