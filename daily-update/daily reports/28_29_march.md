# Research Brief: Danish Government Formation — March 29, 2026

## Summary

The 48 hours of March 28–29 have been a deliberate **negotiation pause** following the first round of sættemøder on Friday March 27. The most significant public development was Mette Frederiksen's Instagram statement on Saturday acknowledging that *"der er ikke en oplagt regering rundt om hjørnet"* (there is no obvious government around the corner). The Sunday March 29 meeting originally scheduled was **cancelled**; next formal talks resume **Tuesday March 31 and Wednesday April 1 at Marienborg**. Behind the scenes, informal bilateral soundings continue. The core deadlock remains unchanged: Moderaterne refuse to underwrite a government whose policies are anchored by Enhedslisten, while SF insists on government participation and will topple any cabinet it is excluded from. Analyst consensus (Hans Engell, Rikke Gjøl Mansø, Pernille Skipper) points to **S+SF+RV+M** as the most probable government constellation — but the EL–M policy gap on wealth tax and economic reform makes this configuration extraordinarily difficult to assemble.

## Developments

### 1. Frederiksen breaks silence with sober Instagram message

**What:** On Saturday morning March 28, Mette Frederiksen posted a personal statement on Instagram — her first extended public comment since the March 27 sættemøder concluded. The post was picked up by Ritzau and published in Kristeligt Dagblad at 12:47.

**Quote:** *"Det politiske landskab lige nu ser svært ud. Og der er ikke en oplagt regering rundt om hjørnet. Jeg forventer lange og svære forhandlinger. Og om det kan lade sig gøre, ved jeg ikke."* She added: *"Jeg ved dog, at vores land har brug for en regering, der kan lede os igennem svære tider. Med uro i verden, krig og potentielt økonomisk tilbageslag bør vi politikere kunne samarbejde og finde en vej."* — Mette Frederiksen, Instagram, March 28, 2026 (via Ritzau/Kristeligt Dagblad)

**Coalition impact:** Frederiksen's tone — unusually candid about the possibility of failure — signals that Day 1 of sættemøder did not produce a breakthrough. Her emphasis on international crises (the Iran war, economic downturn) aligns with the newly announced meeting agenda for next week, which will focus on *"dansk økonomi og Iran-krigens konsekvenser i forhold til priser, vækst og beskæftigelse."* This framing may be a strategic move to create common ground with Moderaterne, whose leader Lars Løkke Rasmussen demanded a new economic plan reflecting the geopolitical situation.

---

### 2. Sunday March 29 meeting cancelled — talks pushed to Marienborg

**What:** Late Friday evening (March 27), Socialdemokratiet announced that the sættemøde originally planned for Sunday March 29 would not take place. The next formal meetings were rescheduled to **Tuesday March 31 and Wednesday April 1 at Marienborg** (the prime minister's country residence). Bilateral meetings with North Atlantic (Greenlandic and Faroese) Folketing members were added to the Tuesday schedule. The weekend was designated for *"opsamling på møderne samt uformelle og bilaterale sonderinger med partierne"* (debriefing and informal bilateral soundings).

**Quote:** *"Det har været konstruktive drøftelser, og de kommende dage bruges nu til opsamling på møderne samt uformelle og bilaterale sonderinger med partierne."* — Socialdemokratiet press release, Friday March 27 evening

**Coalition impact:** The cancellation of the Sunday meeting and shift to Marienborg is tactically significant. Marienborg is the traditional venue for substantive coalition negotiations (as opposed to the introductory sættemøder held at Statsministeriet). This signals the process is transitioning from introductory talks to **real policy discussions**. The delay also gives Frederiksen time to hold informal bilateral contacts — critically, with Lars Løkke Rasmussen — away from cameras and press scrums.

---

### 3. Løkke's Friday evening diagnosis frames the weekend impasse

**What:** After the longest meeting of Day 1 (approximately two hours with Frederiksen), Lars Løkke Rasmussen delivered a detailed doorstep statement Friday evening that effectively defined the negotiation terrain for the weekend pause. He diagnosed a three-way deadlock: SF blocks Venstre and Konservative from government, V and KF block SF and refuse Frederiksen as PM, and Frederiksen insists on the prime ministership. He declared the wealth tax *"absolut no-go"* and said Frederiksen's current mandate gives Enhedslisten an unacceptable anchor role in government policy.

**Quote:** *"Danskerne har sammensat Folketinget på en måde, hvor der hverken er et rødt eller blåt flertal. Moderaterne ligger i midten, og derfor synes vi, at det rigtige ville være, at partier til højre og til venstre for os tog et fælles ansvar."* He warned: *"Med Mette Frederiksens nuværende mandat som kongelig undersøger er det sådan set Enhedslisten, der har ankeret i den politik, en ny regering skal føre. Det risikerer at tegne nogle røde linjer op, som gør, at man ikke kan gøre det, vi synes, Danmark har brug for."* — Lars Løkke Rasmussen, doorstep at Statsministeriet, March 27 (DR, TV2)

**Coalition impact:** Løkke's statement is the most consequential signal of the entire weekend period. He **has not walked away** from negotiations — he explicitly said *"det er alt for tidligt at snakke om nyvalg"* — but he has laid down hard preconditions. His insistence that parties *"til højre og til venstre"* share responsibility points toward a broad midterregering rather than joining a red-bloc-dependent government. The formueskat being declared *"100 procent"* dead removes a key S campaign promise but also removes a major obstacle to M participation. The EL anchor critique is Løkke's primary lever to demand changes in the negotiation framework.

---

### 4. Enhedslisten signals flexibility on process but holds policy ground

**What:** Pelle Dragsted's statements from the March 27 sættemøde — which set the terms for weekend bilateral soundings — showed Enhedslisten striking a careful balance. He acknowledged the vast policy distance to Løkke but expressed willingness to negotiate, framing the burden of compromise as falling primarily on Moderaterne. Crucially, he pushed back on Løkke's claim that EL's main board (hovedbestyrelse) would hold veto power over government policy.

**Quote:** *"Det er Lars Løkke, der gerne vil i regering og skal bruge mine mandater. Det er først og fremmest Lars Løkke, der skal banke på hos mig og forsøge at overbevise mig om, at det er en god idé."* On the main board issue: *"Nej. Vi inddrager vores bagland, og det håber jeg også, at andre partier gør, men det er folketingsgruppen, der forestår forhandlingerne."* After meeting with Frederiksen: *"Det var et rigtig godt møde, og der var en vilje til at finde en vej sammen."* — Pelle Dragsted, various statements March 27 (DR, TV2)

**Coalition impact:** Dragsted's rebuttal of the hovedbestyrelse argument is tactically important — it removes one of Løkke's stated objections. However, EL's hard red line from the kongerunde remains: no blue parties (V, KF, LA, DF, DD, BP) in government. This means EL would tolerate M in government but not V or KF. The S+SF+RV+M constellation therefore remains technically compatible with EL's stated position, provided the policy framework is acceptable to EL.

---

### 5. SF's Pia Olsen Dyhr introduces nyvalg (snap election) as backstop

**What:** In her doorstep statement before entering the March 27 sættemøde, Pia Olsen Dyhr made the unusual move of publicly floating a new election just three days after the previous one — a signal of how seriously SF views the negotiation deadlock.

**Quote:** *"Jeg er et tålmodigt menneske, og hvis det hele brænder sammen — det håber jeg vitterligt ikke, at det gør — så er jeg også klar på at tage et nyvalg, hvis det er det, der skal til."* She also stated: *"Enten er SF i regering, eller også er vi i opposition."* — Pia Olsen Dyhr, March 27 (DR, TV2)

**Coalition impact:** SF's **absolute insistence on government participation** is the hardest structural constraint in the formation process. With 20 seats, SF's no-confidence threat (*"SF vil stemme for et mistillidsvotum til en regering, som SF ikke er en del af"*) means any government without SF needs 90 mandates from the remaining parties — arithmetically very difficult. The nyvalg mention, while likely tactical posturing, creates pressure on Løkke: if negotiations collapse, SF-friendly voters could consolidate further, weakening M's position in a hypothetical re-election. SF's policy priorities from Day 1 included klasseloft (class size caps), rent drikkevand (clean drinking water), and svineproduktion reform.

---

### 6. Venstre and Konservative reject current framework but leave doors ajar

**What:** Both V and KF attended Friday's sættemøder — an important signal in itself — but emerged publicly skeptical. Troels Lund Poulsen (V) said plainly that V cannot be part of an S+SF+RV government. Mona Juul (KF) said *"det ser svært ud"* but notably did not slam the door completely, saying *"vi opfatter, at der bliver lyttet."*

**Quote (Troels Lund Poulsen):** *"Den kongelige undersøger er i gang med at afsøge, om der kan laves en rød regering med SF, Socialdemokratiet og Det Radikale Venstre. Det er det opdrag, den kongelige undersøger har, og det siger sig selv, at sådan en regering kan vi jo ikke være en del af."* — March 27, TV2

**Quote (Mona Juul):** *"De Konservative kan ikke se sig selv i det samarbejde, der arbejdes på nu."* But also: *"Vi opfatter, at der bliver lyttet. Og det er det."* — March 27, TV2

**Coalition impact:** V's categorical rejection of the S+SF+RV formula effectively kills the "monster-midterregering" (S+V+M+RV+KF = 93 seats) as an immediate possibility, though DR analysis notes the idea circulates in corridors. KF's slightly softer tone keeps the door open a crack for a broader arrangement if the framework changes. Notably, a TV2 survey of 115 local Venstre figures found **nearly half** supported governing with S again — suggesting the leadership's hard line may face internal pressure over time.

### 7. Borgernes Parti internal crisis weakens blue bloc margins

**What:** On March 28, Borgernes Parti expelled newly elected MP Jacob Harris over allegations of *"groft uforsvarlig forretningsførelse"* (grossly irresponsible business conduct) — a record for fastest exclusion of a newly elected member (four days post-election). Harris was replaced by Sanne Pilgaard. Separately, Liberal Alliance expelled newly elected MP Cecilie Liv Hansen on March 29.

**Quote:** No direct quote from Lars Boje Mathiesen on the expulsion was found in the sources reviewed.

**Coalition impact:** BP's internal chaos further marginalizes the party. BP entered the Folketing with 4 seats and was the only party to submit a blank recommendation during the kongerunde. Troels Lund Poulsen had already stated *"at invitere Lars Boje i regering kommer ikke til at ske."* The blue bloc's already insufficient 77-seat total becomes even more fragile if BP's remaining members are unreliable. Combined with DF's categorical refusal to work with Moderaterne, a pure blue government remains arithmetically and politically impossible.

### 8. Expert consensus crystallizes around S+SF+RV+M — with major caveats

**What:** Across DR, TV2, Altinget, and Berlingske, political commentators and analysts converged on **S+SF+RV+M** as the most likely government configuration, while unanimously warning that the Enhedslisten–Moderaterne policy gap makes it extraordinarily difficult.

**Quote (Hans Engell, TV2):** *"Mit bedste bud er, at det nok ender med en regering, hvor S, SF, Radikale Venstre og Løkke er med. Så kommer det spændende. Hvad vil Enhedslisten sige til det? Vil de sige, at de ikke gider at se på Løkke? Derfor kan det godt gå hen og blive hamrende besværligt."*

**Quote (Rikke Gjøl Mansø, DR):** *"Med det kæmpe forbehold er der lige nu mest energi i en regering med Socialdemokratiet, Radikale, SF og Moderaterne. Den har bare den kæmpe knast i sig, at det vil betyde, at Lars Løkke Rasmussen skal afstemme sin økonomiske politik med Enhedslistens hovedbestyrelse."*

**Quote (Pernille Skipper, TV2):** *"Jeg har svært ved at se noget som helst. Det er det, der er problemet lige nu."* On Løkke joining: *"Det betyder, at Løkke skal sluge den kamel, at han har sagt, at han ikke vil sidde i en regering, der er baseret på mandater fra hverken den yderste venstrefløj eller den yderste højrefløj."*

**Quote (Rune Stubager, Aarhus University):** *"Det bliver nemmere for dem at lade være med det og nærme sig hinanden, hvis dørene er lukket, og vi andre ikke kan følge med i, hvad der sker."* — TV2, ca. March 27

**Coalition impact:** The analyst consensus underscores a paradox: the **most likely** government is also seen as **extremely difficult** to construct. The S+SF+RV+M configuration commands 82 seats as government parties and reaches 98 with EL+ALT external support — a comfortable majority. But the policy bridge between M and EL remains the central engineering challenge. Stubager's point about closed doors is telling: the shift to Marienborg next week, away from the media circus at Statsministeriet, may be precisely the environment needed for genuine compromise.

## Formation stage

Denmark is in **Phase 2 of the kongelig undersøger process** — the transition from introductory sættemøder (completed March 27) to substantive policy negotiations (beginning March 31 at Marienborg). Mette Frederiksen's mandate as kongelig undersøger remains unchanged: she is tasked with forming a government with **SF and Radikale Venstre** participation. No new kongerunde has been called. The formueskat (wealth tax) is effectively dead as a negotiation item. The caretaker SVM government continues in office.

Key process milestones:
- **March 27:** First-round sættemøder completed with all seven invited parties
- **March 28–30:** Informal bilateral soundings (current phase)
- **March 31:** Bilateral meetings with North Atlantic MPs; resumed negotiations at Marienborg focusing on economy and Iran war consequences
- **April 1:** Continued Marienborg negotiations
- **No deadline** has been set for completing the process. In 2022, the SVM government took 42 days to form.

## Key signals to watch

**Løkke's bilateral activity this weekend.** The most critical variable is whether Lars Løkke Rasmussen is conducting informal bilateral contacts with Frederiksen, Dragsted, or blue-bloc leaders during the March 28–30 pause. DR reported that Løkke *"har bestemt ikke opgivet ambitionen om en regering hen over midten og undersøger selv muligheder."* Whether he is sounding out Troels Lund Poulsen on a blue alternative or edging toward accepting the S+SF+RV+M framework will determine the trajectory.

**The Marienborg meetings' policy substance.** The announced focus on economy and the Iran war is strategically loaded. If Frederiksen can construct a shared economic crisis narrative that bridges M and EL positions — emphasizing fiscal responsibility while protecting welfare — it could unlock the deadlock. Watch for whether EL and M are given sequential or simultaneous meetings.

**EL's policy demands.** Pelle Dragsted has signaled process flexibility (folketingsgruppen negotiates, not the hovedbestyrelse), but EL's substantive demands remain undefined beyond the kongerunde statement. Whether Dragsted tables specific asks — and whether any overlap exists with M's economic reform agenda — will become visible next week.

**Venstre's internal dynamics.** With nearly half of surveyed local V figures open to governing with S, there is a tension between Troels Lund Poulsen's public hardline and the grassroots. If the Marienborg talks stall, watch for whether V signals willingness to reconsider the "monster-midterregering" (S+V+M+RV+KF = 93 seats).

**North Atlantic mandates.** The four Greenlandic and Faroese seats (not included in the 175 Danish seats) could become arithmetically significant. Greenlandic parties are coordinating a joint set of demands for Tuesday's bilateral meeting with Frederiksen. Their alignment could shift majority calculations.

**SF-M relationship signals.** No direct bilateral SF-M contact has been publicly reported. Whether Pia Olsen Dyhr and Lars Løkke Rasmussen engage directly — and whether they can find common ground on economic policy — is a key indicator. Analyst Noa Redington (TV2) expressed doubt: *"Der kan ske mirakler. Det kan godt være, at Lars Løkke Rasmussen vågner op en morgen og siger 'det går jo ikke, Solrún. Jeg bliver nødt til at finde tilbage til mine rødder.' Men jeg tvivler på det."*

**The "grøn midterregering" wildcard (S+SF+KF+M+RV).** Politiken analyst Elisabet Svane floated a speculative fifth constellation — S+SF+KF+M+RV — noting that KF lies closer to SF and RV on climate policy than other blue parties. She called it *"et af de vilde gæt"* and *"fuldstændig usandsynlig"* given KF's stated bond with V and SF's resistance to governing with KF. But in a long negotiation process, unexpected alignments become less impossible. Worth monitoring if the primary S+SF+RV+M track stalls.

## Appendix: Additional analyst frameworks

### Redington's "tripolært system" (Politiken, March 28)

Noa Redington's column in Politiken argues that the election has created a **tripolært system** — a political triangle — that supersedes the traditional rød/blå bloc structure. The three poles are:

- **Morten Messerschmidt (DF)** — lower-right pole. Drives other blue parties beyond conventional limits; makes a functional blue majority impossible; indifferent to government participation. *"Alle de andre er bange for ham, fordi han er ligeglad med at komme i regering."*
- **Pelle Dragsted (EL)** — left pole. Not a flashy result but the *right* result: EL's mandates will be decisive for any Frederiksen government. *"Han har undgået ultimative krav til Frederiksen udover noget med rød og grøn."*
- **Lars Løkke Rasmussen (M)** — centre pole. The only party with a centrist-liberal profile that both resists Messerschmidt's pull and blocks the wealth tax/early retirement expansion. *"Moderaterne er ligeledes det eneste parti med noget, som minder om en pinkgrøn profil, der for alvor sætter foden ned."*

Redington's key claim: *"Det er Pelle Dragsted, Lars Løkke Rasmussen og Morten Messerschmidt, der definerer reglerne... Partierne derimellem er på lange strækninger statister."* Frederiksen does not decide the government's fate — Løkke and Dragsted do, while Messerschmidt holds the blue bloc's future.

**Relevance to model:** This framework reinforces the current model structure where Løkke's orientation is the single most consequential variable. It also suggests the analyst should treat Messerschmidt's blocking power as a structural constant (not negotiable) and Dragsted's flexibility as the key swing variable on the red side.

### Elisabet Svane's coalition ranking (Politiken, March 28)

Politiken's political analyst Elisabet Svane offered a ranked list of government constellations:

1. **S+SF+M+RV** (with EL support) — *"Den har mandaterne, men det stritter mellem Moderaterne og Enhedslisten."* On Løkke's motivation: *"magt [er] det krydderi, der får kamelerne til at glide ned."*
2. **S+V+M+KF+RV** (broad centre) — *"Det er den, vi har talt om hele vejen igennem, men Troels Lund Poulsen lukkede for den her til aften."*
3. **S+SF+KF+M+RV** (green centre) — *"Fuldstændig usandsynlig... men det frække spørgsmål i en lang regeringsforhandling er jo, om det kan blive spiseligt for Konservative."*
4. **Blue government with M** — *"Den slog Morten Messerschmidt ned med det samme."*

Svane's ranking aligns closely with the current model output, with S+SF+M+RV as the clear frontrunner and pure blue effectively dead.
