# Research Brief: Danish Government Formation, March 28, 2026

## Summary

Day one of formal coalition talks (sættemøder) concluded on March 27 at Statsministeriet, with kongelig undersøger Mette Frederiksen receiving all seven invited parties. **The single most consequential development was Lars Løkke Rasmussen's two-hour meeting with Frederiksen** — the day's longest — after which he declared the proposed S+SF+R constellation too narrow, rejected any wealth tax as an "absolut no go," and insisted on a broad centrist government. Meanwhile, SF's Pia Olsen Dyhr floated the possibility of nyvalg if talks collapse — a pressure tactic Løkke dismissed as premature. Radikale Venstre and Enhedslisten both emerged optimistic, while Venstre and Konservative signaled they cannot participate in what is currently on the table. The weekend of March 28–30 is designated for informal bilateral soundings, with formal negotiations resuming March 31 at Marienborg. No government is remotely close to forming.

---

## Developments

### 1. Frederiksen completes a marathon of seven sættemøder

**What:** On March 27, Mette Frederiksen held back-to-back introductory meetings at Statsministeriet with all seven invited parties: Enhedslisten (08:30), Alternativet (15:30), Radikale Venstre, SF, Moderaterne, Venstre, and De Konservative. The Social Democrats' press release afterward described the conversations as constructive and announced that the coming days would be used for assessment and informal bilateral soundings.

**Quote:** *"Det har været konstruktive drøftelser og de kommende dage bruges nu til opsamling på møderne samt uformelle og bilaterale sonderinger med partierne."* — Socialdemokratiet pressemeddelelse, March 27 (TV 2 liveblog)

**Coalition impact:** The wide invitation list — including blue-bloc parties (V, K) alongside the red bloc — confirms Frederiksen is probing beyond a narrow S+SF+R constellation. No party walked out, though V and K attended primarily out of courtesy.

---

### 2. Løkke's two-hour meeting: skeptical but still at the table — and playing for time

**What:** Lars Løkke Rasmussen and a Moderaterne delegation arrived at Statsministeriet around 17:45 and stayed for nearly two hours — by far the longest meeting of the day. Afterward, Løkke delivered a series of pointed statements rejecting dependency on Enhedslisten, dismissing the wealth tax, and insisting on a centrist government formula. Crucially, **he did not walk away from the process.** Politiken's analysis (March 27) describes Løkke's strategy explicitly: he has plenty of time to wait for locked positions to open, with the goal of pressuring parties on both sides to soften — particularly to "vippe Enhedslisten ud af ligningen" (tip Enhedslisten out of the equation as a support party). The strategic logic: not a single Dane should be in doubt that it was difficult to bring Løkke into a government.

**Quotes:**
- On arrival: *"Hun er jo kongelig undersøger og har fået til opdrag at danne en regering med SF og de radikale. Det har vi svært ved rigtig at se det vilde perspektiv i. Men nu kommer vi, først og fremmest for at lytte til, hvilke tanker hun gør sig."* — Lars Løkke Rasmussen, Politiken, March 27
- After the meeting: *"Danskerne har sammensat Folketinget på en måde, hvor der hverken er et rødt eller blåt flertal. Moderaterne ligger i midten, og derfor synes vi, at det rigtige ville være, at partier til højre og til venstre for os tog et fælles ansvar."* — Lars Løkke Rasmussen, DR, March 27

**Coalition impact:** This is the pivotal dynamic. Løkke's continued participation keeps S+SF+M+R alive, but his public rhetoric is designed to extract maximum concessions — particularly on economic policy and the Enhedslisten question. His refusal to join blå blok's preferred path (pointing to himself at the kongerunde rather than Troels Lund Poulsen) means the blue coalition remains blocked.

---

### 3. Wealth tax declared "100 percent dead"

**What:** The formueskat (wealth tax on assets above 10 million DKK), a signature Social Democrat campaign proposal, was effectively killed on Day 1. Løkke called it an "absolut no go" for Moderaterne. Radikale Venstre's Martin Lidegaard had earlier called it "nærmest landsskadelig virksomhed." All blue-bloc parties oppose it. With neither M nor R willing to support it, the policy has no path to a majority.

**Quotes:**
- *"Formueskat er et absolut no go for Moderaterne."* — TV 2 liveblog, March 27
- *"Det nærmer sig landsskadelig virksomhed at indføre en formueskat."* — Martin Lidegaard (R), Børsen via Dansk Erhverv, March 18

**Coalition impact:** This removes one obstacle between S and M but widens the gap between S and its left-flank supporters (Ø, Å) who backed the tax. SF, which championed redistribution, must accept this reality if they want M in government.

---

### 4. SF floats nyvalg and signals readiness for painful compromises

**What:** Pia Olsen Dyhr told journalists she would be ready for a new election if negotiations collapse entirely — a pressure tactic aimed at Moderaterne. Løkke responded with an immediate rebuke. But crucially, **Politiken reports that Dyhr already on election night signaled internally to SF members that the parliamentary, economic, and security-political reality would likely mean painful compromises.** She is managing SF expectations downward while using tough rhetoric outward.

**Quotes:**
- *"Der er ingen tvivl om, at vi kommer til at bøje os mod hinanden. Det bliver meget besværligt."* — Pia Olsen Dyhr, Politiken, March 27
- *"Hvis det hele brænder sammen — det håber jeg virkelig ikke — så er jeg da også klar til at tage et nyvalg, hvis det er det, der skal til."* — Pia Olsen Dyhr, Politiken/Avisen.dk, March 27
- Løkke's reply: *"Det er alt for tidligt at snakke om nyvalg. Det er tre dage siden, der var et folketingsvalg, og sidste gang tog det 42 dage."* — TV 2 liveblog, March 27

**Coalition impact:** The double signal — tough public rhetoric (nyvalg threat) combined with private expectation management (compromise incoming) — suggests SF is preparing its base for significant concessions to accommodate Moderaterne. This marginally increases the probability that SF accepts M in government, **but SF still holds a hard red line: it will vote no-confidence on any government it is not part of.**

---

### 5. Enhedslisten: one ultimatum — reduce inequality

**What:** Pelle Dragsted was first through the door at 08:30 and emerged upbeat. Politiken reports that Enhedslisten has signaled for some time that it does not rule out accommodating Moderaterne in a parliamentary basis. Their single ultimatum: a "forståelsespapir" (understanding paper) containing **concrete steps to reduce inequality in Denmark**. This is a notably flexible position — a single, vaguely operationalized demand rather than a laundry list.

On entry, Dragsted directly targeted Løkke's "noget ikke nogen" branding, calling out what he sees as hypocrisy in Løkke pre-emptively excluding Enhedslisten while claiming to put substance over personalities.

**Quotes:**
- *"Hvis alle er parate til at strække sig mod hinanden, mener jeg, at det kan blive en holdbar regering. Men hvis nogen sætter sig i et hjørne, så har man måske glemt det med, at det handler om 'noget' og ikke 'nogen'."* — Pelle Dragsted, Politiken, March 27
- After the meeting: *"Der er en vilje."* — Pelle Dragsted, TV 2 liveblog
- On the hovedbestyrelse concern: *"Nej. Vi inddrager vores bagland, og det håber jeg også, at andre partier gør, men det er folketingsgruppen, der forestår forhandlingerne."* — Pelle Dragsted, TV 2 liveblog

**Coalition impact:** Dragsted is making Enhedslisten as palatable as possible: emphasizing folketingsgruppe autonomy (not the hovedbestyrelse), accepting M in government, and reducing demands to a single abstract principle (inequality reduction). This matters enormously because S+SF+M+R = ~82 mandater, short of 90 — Enhedslisten's 11 seats are arithmetically necessary.

---

### 6. Radikale Venstre: determined not to repeat the 2022 mistake

**What:** Martin Lidegaard emerged calling the sættemøde "virkelig godt og konstruktivt." Politiken's analysis highlights a crucial institutional memory: Lidegaard's failure to enter government in 2022, when the opportunity was there, caused internal shock and became a defining lesson. Radikale are now absolutely determined to get into government. They can envision two scenarios: a centre-left government (S+SF+R+M) or the so-called "monster-midte" (S+V+M+R+K). Their top priority is a green government. Critically, **Politiken notes that RV does not think Venstre fits a green agenda**, making the most realistic path from Lidegaard's perspective a red-parties-plus-Moderaterne constellation.

**Quotes:**
- On entry: *"Mange har travlt med at udelukke alle mulige andre partier. Det har vi ikke."* — Martin Lidegaard, Politiken, March 27
- After meeting: *"Der er ikke 100 procent enighed om det hele, men der er en retning, som begynder at tegne sig, og som er grøn og mere ansvarlig."* — Martin Lidegaard, TV 2 liveblog
- *"Der kan ikke laves en regering henover midten uden vores mandater. Og selvfølgelig skal vi også være med i den. Alt andet ville være en stor skam."* — Martin Lidegaard, TV 2 liveblog

**Coalition impact:** Radikale positioning themselves as the indispensable bridge. Their openness to multiple configurations but preference for the green path (excluding V) aligns with the S+SF+M+R core scenario. The fact that they reject V for green reasons narrows the viable options.

---

### 7. Venstre: deep personal animosity and structural crisis

**What:** Both Troels Lund Poulsen and the V delegation attended but made clear they see no path into the current S-led constellation. **Politiken provides crucial context on the personal dimension**: V leadership feels S ran an unfair, personal campaign — particularly sending Helle Thorning-Schmidt to revive the old tax case against Troels Lund Poulsen, and attacking him as being in agriculture's pocket. Politiken states bluntly that the old "Stockholmssyndrom" (the tendency for V to be accommodating toward S) has "godt og grundigt fordampet" (evaporated completely).

**Quotes:**
- *"For os er det fuldstændig afgørende, hvad det politiske indhold er. Og dér er det jo meget klart, at vi vil gøre Danmark rigere, fordi vi vil investere mere i forsvar, og vi vil investere mere i kernevelfærd."* — Troels Lund Poulsen, Politiken, March 27
- *"Den kongelige undersøger er i gang med at afsøge, om der kan laves en rød regering med SF, Socialdemokratiet og Radikale Venstre. Det er det opdrag, den kongelige undersøger har, og det siger sig selv, at sådan en regering kan vi jo ikke være en del af."* — Troels Lund Poulsen, Politiken, March 27
- Stephanie Lose: *"Med den politik, Socialdemokratiet er gået til valg på, så har vi svært ved at se for os, at vi har så meget at tale om."* — DR, March 27

**Coalition impact:** The personal animosity dimension — beyond just policy distance — makes V participation in an S-led government significantly less likely than the pure ideological distance would suggest. This is important for the model: V→S bilateral acceptance should be lower than "harshness" alone implies. However, TV 2 reporting indicates that **a majority of V local association chairmen** believe V should be willing to govern with S, and internal discontent is simmering over Troels Lund Poulsen's strategy after the historically bad result.

---

### 8. Konservative: quietly content with likely opposition

**What:** Mona Juul expressed surprise at the narrow mandate but did not slam the door. **Politiken's analysis reveals that internally, Konservative are not unhappy about the prospect of opposition.** They see their best potential scenario as a SVMRK "monster-midte" government, but assess this as unrealistic because V cannot go with S again. Opposition would let them build up a strengthened folketingsgruppe — including three newly elected former KU chairmen — for the next election cycle.

**Quotes:**
- *"Det ser svært ud. Det er også derfor, vi er lidt overraskede over, at den kongelige undersøger er i gang med at lave en regering med S, SF og Radikale Venstre."* — Mona Juul, Politiken, March 27

**Coalition impact:** K's quiet comfort with opposition reduces the probability of the "dark horse" S+SF+M+R+K scenario that would solve the EL dependency problem. If K is not actively seeking government participation, Frederiksen cannot count on them to join simply to avoid Enhedslisten support.

---

### 9. Alternativet: the svinepagt as sole ultimatum

**What:** Franciska Rosenkilde emerged positive from her sættemøde. **Politiken reports that Alternativet has a single ultimative krav: the "svinepagt" — stopping industrial pig production that violates animal welfare law.** Pre-election, nobody wanted to sign it. But after a campaign where pigs (svin) featured prominently, the appetite for accommodating the demand may have grown. Politiken notes that if Å gets concessions on animal welfare and green issues, the party can stretch on other areas — which will be necessary for supporting a government with Moderaterne.

**Quotes:**
- *"Jeg kan danne regeringen der, hvor mine mandater forhåbentlig kommer til at trække en rød regering i en grønnere retning."* — Franciska Rosenkilde, Politiken, March 27
- *"Hvis Moderaterne spiller ind med mere stop for svineeksport, et nationalt sprøjteforbud og væk med moms på frugt og grønt, så er der rigtig meget at arbejde med."* — Franciska Rosenkilde, Politiken, March 27

**Coalition impact:** Å's flexibility beyond the svinepagt makes them a relatively easy support party to satisfy. Their mandates provide extra padding in the S+SF+M+R constellation.

---

### 10. Messerschmidt's Publicistklubben gambit

**What:** At a Publicistklubben event on March 25, Morten Messerschmidt (DF) made a surprise proposal: that both DF and Moderaterne stay out of government and instead serve as støttepartier for a blue V-K-LA-DD government. This was a calculated maneuver to expose Løkke's personal ambitions — if Løkke refused (which he did), it would demonstrate that M's objection to blå blok was about ego, not policy.

**Quotes:**
- *"Lars Løkke, jeg vil tilbyde, at der dannes en blå regering — men uden Moderaterne og uden Dansk Folkeparti. Vi skal være støttepartier for en blå regering."* — Morten Messerschmidt (DF), Publicistklubben, March 25

**Coalition impact:** Løkke rejected the proposal. DF also stated at the kongerunde an absolute demand for "muslimsk netto-udvandring" — a condition no centrist or left-wing party will accept, effectively self-excluding DF from any realistic government.

---

### 11. Vanopslagh and the blue bloc's structural weakness

**What:** Alex Vanopslagh (LA) backed Troels Lund Poulsen as statsminister and pushed for a broad non-socialist government. However, blå blok at 73–77 mandater is mathematically further from 90 than rød blok's 84. The blue path would require M+R to cross 90, but R has oriented decisively toward a green, centre-left direction.

**Quotes:**
- *"Hvis Morten Messerschmidt ikke er klar til at samarbejde med Lars Løkke Rasmussen, så giver han fire år mere til Mette Frederiksen."* — Alex Vanopslagh, DR

**Coalition impact:** Blue bloc remains structurally weaker and internally divided (DF refuses M; DF demands impossible immigration conditions; V is in crisis). A blue coalition is the low-probability tail scenario.

## Commentator consensus

Analysts converge on **S+SF+M+R as a four-party government** with EL and Å as external støttepartier (~98 mandater including support).

- **Hans Engell**: Predicts S+SF+M+R with EL and Å as støttepartier. — TV 2, March 25
- **Rikke Gjøl Mansø** (DR): Notes most energy is in S+R+SF+M, but flags the "kæmpe knast" of Løkke accepting EL as parlamentarisk grundlag. — DR
- **Pernille Skipper**: Skeptical that any constellation satisfies all demands simultaneously. — TV 2
- **Søs Marie Serup**: Predicts ~1 month to government formation.
- **Christine Cordsen** (DR): If everyone holds firm, nothing works.

## Formation stage

**Current stage: Early exploratory (sættemøder completed → bilateral sonderinger)**

Mette Frederiksen remains **kongelig undersøger** with a mandate to form a government with SF and Radikale Venstre participation. The first round of seven sættemøder was completed March 27. The weekend of March 28–30 is a pause for internal assessment and informal bilateral soundings. Formal negotiations resume **March 31 and April 1 at Marienborg**, where the agenda includes the economy and Iran war consequences for prices, growth, and employment. Bilateral meetings with North Atlantic Folketing members are also scheduled for March 31.

---

## Key signals to watch

**1. Løkke's movement on the EL question.** The decisive variable. His strategy (per Politiken) is explicitly to use time to "mørne" parties and "vippe Enhedslisten ud." Watch for any softening at the Marienborg meetings — or alternatively, Frederiksen finding an alternative path that satisfies Løkke's objection (e.g., a very narrow forståelsespapir with EL, or pulling K in).

**2. Venstre's internal crisis.** A majority of V local chairmen favor willingness to govern with S, while Troels Lund is anchored by personal animosity. If internal pressure mounts, a broader cross-centre formula could emerge — but V participation remains unlikely given the "Stockholmssyndrom fordampet" dynamic Politiken describes.

**3. SF's willingness to compromise.** Dyhr is already managing her base's expectations downward (election night signal about "svære kompromiser"). The question is how far SF will bend on economic policy and EL's role. SF's hard line: government participation is non-negotiable.

**4. The Marienborg meetings (March 31–April 1).** First substantive policy discussions. The stated agenda — economy and the Iran war — is designed to build common ground on crisis management, a domain where centrist pragmatism could bridge the SF-M gap.

**5. Konservative's posture.** Politiken's analysis that K is quietly content with opposition reduces the S+SF+M+R+K dark-horse scenario's probability. But if Frederiksen makes a sufficiently compelling offer, K's newly elected members might push for relevance.

**6. Informal bilateral channels over the weekend (March 28–30).** The most important conversations may happen off-camera. Watch for leaks from Frederiksen-Løkke contacts.

**7. Enhedslisten's forståelsespapir.** The content of what EL demands in its understanding paper — and how abstract vs. specific the inequality-reduction commitments are — will determine whether Løkke can stomach it. A vague commitment might be acceptable; a binding wealth-redistribution target would not.
