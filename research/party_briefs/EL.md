# Enhedslisten: the indispensable outsider shaping Denmark's 2026 coalition math

Enhedslisten (EL) emerges from the 24 March 2026 election with **enhanced leverage but constrained bargaining power** — a party whose mandates are mathematically necessary for any left-leaning government yet whose maximalist demands face structural impossibility. The party gained seats (to roughly 11, at **6.3%**), part of a historic left-of-S surge, while the SVM governing parties were punished. EL's primary lever remains the **forståelsespapir mechanism**: a written cooperation agreement with the governing party, without which EL's budget-vote record shows near-total opposition. The central tension in government formation is that any viable majority almost certainly requires both Moderaterne's 14 seats *and* EL's tolerance — yet Løkke has vowed not to depend on the "far left," and EL demands a "red government" excluding Moderaterne. This structural catch-22 is the defining puzzle of the 2026 formation process.

## A party transformed on security, sharpened on welfare

The 2022–2026 opposition period produced the most dramatic ideological evolution in Enhedslisten's history — but the shift was asymmetric. On foreign and security policy, the transformation was revolutionary; on domestic policy, the party hardened its left-wing profile.

**The security revolution** unfolded across successive annual meetings. In 2023, EL shelved its long-standing demand for NATO exit, declaring it "not current." In 2024, it wrote NATO acceptance into its *principprogram* (158–125 vote) and — for the first time — accepted Denmark having a military, replacing the call to "abolish" it with support for *territorialforsvar* (territorial defense). By the 2025 annual meeting, a comprehensive security policy document was adopted with roughly 70% support, confirming defense spending increases, continued NATO membership, and Nordic security cooperation. The party still opposes the **5% of GDP** defense target, US military bases on Danish soil, and nuclear weapons — but the core pacifist identity is gone.

This shift triggered real internal costs. The **Rødt Venstre faction** (~100 members) was marginalized at an extraordinary December 2024 meeting after protesting "right-wing drift." The youth wing **SUF** severed its 24-year cooperation with EL in October 2025 over NATO and defense. The far-left marxist.dk called Dragsted's government ambitions "the drop that makes the cup overflow." Despite this, the majority held.

On domestic policy, EL sharpened rather than softened. Pelle Dragsted, who became political spokesperson in August 2023, framed the party as defending "*fællesskabsdanmark*" against a government serving "*Overdanmark*" — elite Denmark. He attacked SVM relentlessly: tax cuts for property speculators and corporate heirs, a *kontanthjælpsreform* that allegedly pushed 9,000 children deeper into poverty, abolition of Store Bededag, insufficient climate action. His **"Nordisk socialisme"** framework — socialism as expansion of existing cooperative, public, and democratic ownership traditions — provided intellectual scaffolding for pragmatism without ideological surrender.

## Campaign demands ranged from wealth taxes to rent caps

EL's 2026 campaign, branded **"Et Danmark du har råd til"** (A Denmark You Can Afford), was a cost-of-living offensive anchored by several major policy packages.

The headline demand was the **formueskat (wealth tax)**: a 1% annual tax on net wealth above **35 million DKK**, covering liquid assets, securities, unlisted shares, housing, and pension savings minus debt. On 25 February, Dragsted told Berlingske this was an *"ultimativt krav"* for supporting a red government. Socialdemokratiet subsequently proposed its own softer version (0.5% above 25M DKK), and over 500 business leaders signed an open letter against it.

The **"Solidarisk skattereform"** (12 March 2026) proposed raising the *personfradrag* (personal allowance) by 12,000 DKK for incomes under 300,000 DKK, financed by taxing capital gains at the same rate as earned income — projected to yield **13.2 billion DKK** annually. Additional proposals included halving food VAT entirely, eliminating VAT on fruit and vegetables, free prescription medicine (**4.1 billion DKK/year**), free dental care (**8.5 billion DKK/year**), and a **Danmarksbillet** at 399 DKK/month for unlimited public transport.

On climate, EL pushed for **90% CO₂ reduction by 2035** and climate neutrality by 2040 — more ambitious than existing targets. A joint demand with SF, Radikale, and Alternativet called for a binding **nature and biodiversity law** (30% protected land and sea). EL proposed a total pesticide ban on vulnerable groundwater areas, a *svinestop* (ban on expanding pig factories), and 100% organic agriculture as a long-term goal.

On housing, EL proposed a national **huslejeloft (rent cap)** in pressured urban areas, a **15% tax on housing capital gains** exceeding 1 million DKK, expanded *almene boliger* (social housing), and abolition of the *Ghettolov*.

Immigration remained EL's most liberal and most internally contentious position. The party maintained demands for more quota refugees, easier citizenship paths, reversal of convention-violating deportation policies, and abolition of the *Ghettolov*. A TV2 investigation (19 March 2026) found **7 of 19 local EL contacts** said they could not support a government continuing S's hard immigration line. Yet group chair Peder Hvelplund notably declined to make immigration an absolute red line, acknowledging it "kan være svært at flytte på" since a majority from SF to the far right supports the current stance.

## Red government or nothing — but what counts as "red"?

EL's coalition stance is organized around a single, firm structural demand and several negotiable policy demands. The structural demand: there must be a **forståelsespapir** (written cooperation agreement). Peder Hvelplund stated on 19 March: *"Enhedslisten har kun ét ultimativt krav til regeringsforhandlinger. Og det er faktisk, at der kommer et forhandlet grundlag."* The content is negotiable; the framework's existence is not.

Beyond this, EL drew a bright line against supporting either "blue governments to the right" or "gray governments in the center" — a formulation from Dragsted's own profile page that explicitly targets any SVM 2.0 arrangement. The party demands S pursue a *rød regering* (red government) as a precondition for EL pointing to Frederiksen as *kongelig undersøger*. In December 2025, EL even stated it preferred an SF Prime Minister over an S one, though SF declined to pursue this.

The critical ambiguity concerns **Moderaterne**. EL's pre-election position (articulated by Mai Villadsen on DR) flatly ruled out any government including M. But on election night, the language shifted subtly: Hvelplund demanded Frederiksen *"afsøge muligheden for en rød regering"* (explore the possibility of a red government) — not that she categorically exclude M. This phrasing leaves the door ajar for eventually tolerating M's presence if the exploration "fails" and the policy price is right.

At the historic 2025 annual meeting, Dragsted made an unprecedented declaration: **EL wants to enter government**. DR called this a *"nybrud"* (breakthrough). This aspiration remains largely theoretical for 2026 given the arithmetic and M's veto, but it signals long-term strategic evolution away from permanent opposition.

## Election night: EL withheld support, then likely relented

On election night (24 March), EL's leadership delivered calibrated conditional statements. Dragsted, at the party's election event: *"Første slag står i morgen. Vores krav er de samme. Vi peger kun på en forhandlingsleder, der ønsker en rød regering. Og jeg har ikke hørt Mette Frederiksen sige, at hun vil det."* Hvelplund reinforced this: *"Det er klart, så kan vi ikke pege på hende som forhandlingsleder"* if Frederiksen doesn't commit within hours to pursuing a red government.

This was a **leverage play, not a permanent veto**. Dragsted's own framing revealed the logic: *"Jeg kan næsten ikke forestille mig, at Mette Frederiksen, hvis danskerne vælger et rød-grønt flertal, skulle sige 'nej, det vil jeg ikke bruge.'"* He was betting that Frederiksen would eventually commit to exploring a red-leaning government rather than risk losing EL's mandates. By the following day (25 March), an Altinget headline — *"Mette Frederiksen er valgt til kongelig undersøger"* — indicated Frederiksen was indeed chosen, suggesting EL either pointed to her or did not block the appointment.

On the same day, **SF, EL, Radikale, and Alternativet** held a coordinated joint press conference demanding a binding nature and biodiversity law — signaling red bloc unity and coordinated leverage going into negotiations.

EL's view of **Løkke as kingmaker** is deeply skeptical. Mai Villadsen's earlier statement captures the sentiment: *"Jeg har brugt det meste af mit politiske liv på at bekæmpe Lars Løkkes dårlige ideer."* The structural problem is mutual: Løkke has repeatedly said he will not sit in a government dependent on the "yderste venstrefløj." Former EL leader and TV2 commentator Pernille Skipper crystallized the paradox: *"Løkke skal sluge den kamel, at han har sagt, at han ikke vil sidde i en regering, der er baseret på mandater fra hverken den yderste venstrefløj eller den yderste højrefløj."*

## Experts see essential leverage, questionable red lines

Political commentators converge on the assessment that EL holds **structurally necessary but substantively constrained** leverage. Hans Engell, the veteran political commentator, predicted the most likely outcome: an **S + SF + M + R government** with EL and Alternativet as the two external support parties. This configuration would require a forståelsespapir.

TV2's Noa Redington delivered the sharpest assessment of EL's stated demands: the wealth tax is *"stendød"* (stone dead), opposed not only by the blue bloc and Moderaterne but also by Radikale Venstre. *"Statsministeren har slet ikke talt om formueskatten de seneste uger."* This gap between EL's "ultimativt krav" and political reality is the clearest credibility test for EL's bargaining position.

Altinget's Erik Holstein offered a more nuanced reading of EL's flexibility: *"Behind the hard rhetoric... lies a good portion of pragmatism."* He noted EL's approach has historically alternated between aggressive public positioning and realistic deal-making. The 2019 precedent confirms this — EL accepted a forståelsespapir that made no meaningful concessions on immigration, the issue its base cares most about.

Dragsted himself is widely viewed as **the most strategically sophisticated leader EL has had**. DR profiled him as the figure who took EL from "autonomous activist" to "broad popular party, perhaps even government-ready." His 2019 candid assessment is revealing: *"We are surprised at how much we got through... This represents a very pragmatic role, which perhaps surprises many."* On green transition skeptics, he showed unusual self-criticism: *"I've been too quick to think it's just people who are against the green transition... That it's just Inger Støjberg. But it's been a huge mistake on my part."*

## The forståelsespapir is EL's only real weapon — and it works

The forståelsespapir mechanism is EL's primary instrument of political influence, and the party's budget-vote record demonstrates its binary logic with remarkable clarity. **During 2019–2022**, when EL had a forståelsespapir with the S-led government (*"Retfærdig retning for Danmark"*), the party voted for every single finance bill. **During 2022–2026**, without a forståelsespapir, EL voted against every single finance bill — becoming, by Finanslov 2026, the only party outside the agreement. This on/off pattern is the clearest behavioral signal in the dataset: **the forståelsespapir is the switch**.

The 2019 forståelsespapir was an 18-page document committing S to a binding climate law (70% CO₂ reduction by 2030), legally binding minimum staffing in daycare, measures against inequality and child poverty, resumed quota refugee intake, and respect for international conventions. Dragsted called the outcome surprisingly favorable. But frustrations accumulated: Radio4 counted **29 immigration tightenings** versus 15 relaxations under S, and EL members described the paper's promises as having *"the same durability as elastic in tape measures."*

For 2026, EL has incorporated these lessons. Rosa Lund stated it was a *"mistake"* not to set **negative demands** — things S must NOT do — in 2019. The party now wants measurable criteria and explicit prohibitions alongside positive commitments. Specific documented demands include the wealth tax, inequality reduction commitments, a binding nature law, a pesticide ban on groundwater areas, marine environment protections, worker protections, and — critically — a commitment not to violate international conventions.

The **EL-M conditioning** is the most consequential variable for the model. If Moderaterne enters government alongside S (recreating a *midterregering*), EL becomes less willing but not necessarily unwilling to provide budget support — provided the forståelsespapir price is right. EL has not formally vetoed supporting a government that includes M. Hvelplund's election-night demand was that Frederiksen *explore* a red government, not that M be permanently excluded. The most likely equilibrium, per expert consensus, is precisely this: an S+SF+M+R government where EL acts as external support party with a forståelsespapir, tolerating M's presence in exchange for policy concessions on welfare, climate, and inequality. The wealth tax will likely not survive, but alternative inequality measures (capital gains taxation, housing gains tax) could substitute. Immigration remains the deepest tension — leadership is pragmatic, but **grassroots pressure is a genuine constraint**, not mere bluster.

## What the model should encode

For coalition simulation purposes, four behavioral parameters define EL's positioning:

- **Budget support is binary on forståelsespapir existence.** Without one, EL's baseline is opposition to the finance bill. With one, EL has historically supported every budget. This should be modeled as a hard switch.
- **The forståelsespapir content threshold is lower than stated demands suggest.** EL's opening positions (wealth tax, immigration reform) are substantially more ambitious than what it will accept. The 2019 precedent shows EL accepted a deal with essentially zero immigration concessions. Expect similar pragmatism on the wealth tax — alternative inequality measures will suffice.
- **M's presence in government raises EL's price but does not trigger an absolute veto.** EL is less enthusiastic but not categorically opposed to supporting a government that includes Moderaterne. The conditioning should be modeled as an increased policy-concession threshold, not a binary exclusion.
- **Grassroots constraints on immigration create a genuine floor.** Unlike the wealth tax (which leadership can trade away), immigration is an area where EL's decentralized, democratic structure creates bottom-up pressure that limits leadership flexibility. A 2026 forståelsespapir will almost certainly include stronger "negative demands" — prohibitions on convention violations — as a minimum grassroots-satisfying concession.
