# DF/DD Blue Tolerance Research

## Summary

The direct post-election evidence does **not** support the claim that DF or DD would easily let a **V-led government including Moderaterne** take office, especially not without major concessions. DF’s strongest formal statement after the election says the opposite: in the official kongerunde summary on **25 March 2026**, DF backed Troels Lund Poulsen as negotiator **on the condition** that a blue government **not include Moderaterne**. On the same day, Messerschmidt floated a narrower compromise in debate: DF and Moderaterne could both remain **outside** government and support a blue government from Parliament. That is evidence of possible **tactical tolerance** of a V-led government **without** DF in cabinet and **without** M in cabinet, but it is **not** evidence that DF would tolerate an **M-inclusive** government. DD is more open in form, but the direct evidence is thinner: DD’s formal **25 March 2026** kongerunde line backed Troels Lund for a blue government with broad policy aims, but I found **no direct post-election DD statement** saying it would tolerate a **V-led, M-inclusive** government, and no direct post-election DD statement explicitly ruling it out either. On the evidence below, **DF tolerance of an M-inclusive V-led government looks weak; DD tolerance is ambiguous but unproven; and “free tolerance” is unsupported for both.** ([kongehuset.dk][1])

## DF tolerance posture

### Statements found

The strongest **formal** DF statement is the kongerunde text from **25 March 2026**. DF did not merely say “we want blue.” It said Troels Lund should lead talks **provided** the outcome is a blue government that excludes **both** Socialdemokratiet **and Moderaterne**. That is direct post-election evidence on government formation, and it is directly about Moderaterne’s participation. ([kongehuset.dk][1])

The strongest countervailing item is also from **25 March 2026**. In the post-election leaders’ debate, Messerschmidt publicly offered Lars Løkke a different arrangement: both DF and Moderaterne could stay **outside** government and support a blue Venstre-led government as **støttepartier**. That is real evidence that DF could imagine **tolerating** a government it was not itself part of. But it is **not** evidence that DF would tolerate **Moderaterne inside the cabinet**. It points instead to a model where M is kept out of government and reduced to a support-party role. ([dknyt.dk][2])

### Conditions/demands

DF’s formal **25 March 2026** condition set is substantial, not cosmetic. It tied support for Troels Lund to a blue government that would pursue **“muslimsk netto-udvandring”** and would **exclude Moderaterne from participation**. That is direct evidence **against** “free tolerance.” Even on the narrow question of letting a government take office, DF’s own formal position already attached major policy and composition conditions. ([kongehuset.dk][1])

### Signals about M

The post-election evidence on M is restrictive, not permissive. DF’s formal kongerunde position excludes Moderaterne from government, while Messerschmidt’s softer public overture still places Moderaterne **outside** government. In other words, the strongest direct DF evidence allows at most **coexistence as parallel support parties**, not **joint cabinet participation**. ([kongehuset.dk][1])

### No-evidence items

* **No direct post-election DF statement found** saying DF would tolerate a **V-led government with Moderaterne in government**.
* **No direct post-election DF statement found** offering **durable parliamentary support**, budget support, or finance-act support to such a government.
* **No direct post-election DF statement found** saying DF would do this **without major concessions**. The evidence found points the other way. ([kongehuset.dk][1])

## DD tolerance posture

### Statements found

DD’s clearest post-election statement is the kongerunde summary from **25 March 2026**. DD backed Troels Lund as royal investigator with the aim of forming **“en borgerlig regering”** and attached a recognizable policy profile: lower taxes, stricter immigration, better land-by balance, and a more common-sense green transition. That is direct post-election evidence of willingness to let a blue route be explored. ([kongehuset.dk][1])

### Conditions/demands

DD’s formal **25 March 2026** position did not name Moderaterne, but it did not offer blank-cheque tolerance either. It described a blue government with specific ambitions on taxes, immigration, rural-urban balance, and green policy. That is weaker and more open-ended than DF’s condition set, but it still looks like a **programmatic demand profile**, not “free tolerance.” ([kongehuset.dk][1])

### Signals about M

In the direct post-election sources reviewed here, DD did **not** set out a clear public line on whether it would tolerate a **V-led government that includes Moderaterne**. The formal kongerunde text is silent on that specific point. ([kongehuset.dk][1])

### No-evidence items

* **No direct post-election Støjberg/DD statement found** saying DD would tolerate a **V-led government that includes Moderaterne**.
* **No direct post-election Støjberg/DD statement found** explicitly saying DD would **block** a V-led government **because** it includes Moderaterne.
* **No direct post-election DD statement found** offering **free tolerance without major concessions**.
* **No direct post-election DD statement found** promising durable support, budget support, or finance-act support to an M-inclusive blue government. ([kongehuset.dk][1])

## Historical precedent

The most relevant precedent from **2022–2025** is not that DF or DD once tolerated an M-inclusive blue government; I found **no such precedent** in the sources reviewed here. What the record instead shows is that Moderaterne’s project has consistently been **over the middle**. In the **2 November 2022** kongerunde, Moderaterne backed Mette Frederiksen as the figure to explore **“en regering hen over Folketingets midte.”** In **June 2024**, Moderaterne was still publicly presenting its governing project as **“En regering henover midten.”** ([kongehuset.dk][3])

For DD, the **2 November 2022** kongerunde showed a straightforward blue preference: DD wanted Inger Støjberg herself as royal investigator for a blue government with a familiar immigration/decentralization/welfare/tax profile. That supports the proposition that DD is structurally more at home in blue than M is. But it still does **not** show willingness to tolerate an **M-inclusive** blue government. ([kongehuset.dk][3])

## Commentator analysis

Serious commentary points to a constraint that is broader than “would DF/DD be blue enough?” The Omni summary of **25 March 2026** reports that TV2’s Hans Redder found a blue middle government hard to imagine because it would require cooperation with Messerschmidt, while DR’s Christine Cordsen said Løkke had refused a government that includes or depends on DF. If that summary is accurate, the obstacle is **not only** DF/DD tolerance; it is also **Moderaterne’s own orientation and Løkke’s aversion to DF dependence**. ([Omni][5])

## Assessment

**How plausible is DF tolerance?**
A limited form of DF tolerance is **plausible**: DF clearly wanted Troels Lund to explore a blue government on **25 March 2026**, and Messerschmidt even floated a setup where DF could stay **outside** government and still support it. But the direct evidence also shows that DF’s tolerance is **heavily conditioned**, and the strongest formal evidence says that a blue government must **not include Moderaterne**. So DF tolerance of a generic blue government is plausible; DF tolerance of a **V-led government with M inside cabinet** is **weakly supported at best, and more likely contradicted by the strongest direct evidence**. ([kongehuset.dk][1])

**How plausible is DD tolerance?**
DD tolerance of **some** blue government is plausible. Its formal **25 March 2026** statement backed Troels Lund and laid out a blue policy direction. But DD’s position is much less specific than DF’s. On the crucial question — whether DD would let a **V-led, M-inclusive** government take office while DD stayed outside — the direct post-election record here is simply **thin**. That makes DD look **more open than DF**, but not clearly committed. ([kongehuset.dk][1])

**How plausible is tolerance of a V-led government that includes Moderaterne?**
On the evidence here, this is the **least supported** part of the assumption. For DF, the strongest direct post-election evidence is a formal **no** to Moderaterne being part of such a government. For DD, I found **no direct post-election yes**. The best evidence for any DF–M coexistence is Messerschmidt’s support-party formula, which specifically keeps both parties **out of government**. That is materially different from tolerating an **M-inclusive cabinet**. ([kongehuset.dk][1])

**Which parts of the current assumption look strongest?**
The strongest part is that **DD might, in principle, tolerate a blue formation route from outside government**, because its formal line is broad and non-exclusive. A second strong part is that **DF could tolerate a V-led government in a very narrow sense** if DF itself stayed out and M also stayed out. ([kongehuset.dk][1])

**Which parts look weakest or unsupported?**
The weakest part is the claim that **DF and DD would let a V-led government including Moderaterne take office without major concessions**. For DF, the evidence points against that. For DD, there is no direct post-election evidence establishing it. The broader realism problem also does not sit only with DF/DD: the commentary and historical record suggest that **Moderaterne/Løkke’s own position** is part of why this route looks difficult. ([kongehuset.dk][1])
